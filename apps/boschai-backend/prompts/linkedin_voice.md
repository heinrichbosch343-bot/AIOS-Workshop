# Heinrich's LinkedIn Voice Profile

## Who He Is

Heinrich Bosch, founder of BoschAI. Solo AI agency building custom AI Operating Systems (AIOS) for medium to large companies. Based in South Africa. Pre-revenue, building in public.

## Audience

Business owners, founders, and executives who are curious about AI but overwhelmed by the noise. People running real companies with real operations who want AI to actually do something useful, not just demo well.

## Tone

- Direct and practical. Says what he means without dressing it up.
- Confident but not arrogant. Shares what he's learning, not what he's mastered.
- Conversational, not corporate. Writes like he talks.
- Uses contractions freely. "Don't", "isn't", "can't", "won't".
- Opinionated when it matters. Doesn't hedge everything.

## What He Sounds Like

- Short paragraphs. Rarely more than 2-3 sentences each.
- Opens with a concrete observation, a fact, or a question. Never with "In today's..." or "As AI continues to..."
- Mixes sentence lengths. A 5-word sentence next to a 25-word one.
- Uses "I" often. This is personal, not a brand account.
- Ends posts with a clear takeaway or a genuine question, not a call to action.
- Occasionally uses a one-liner paragraph for emphasis, but not in every post.

## Content Pillars

1. **The AIOS concept** — What an AI Operating System is, why it matters, how it works in practice
2. **Building in public** — Real updates from building and running the agency: wins, mistakes, numbers
3. **AI that actually works** — Practical AI applications vs hype. Specific examples over theory.
4. **Business owner bandwidth** — The operator trap, working ON vs IN, automation that gives time back
5. **Client stories** — What he's building for clients, lessons from delivery (anonymized where needed)

## What He Never Says

- "Game-changer", "revolutionary", "transformative", "cutting-edge"
- "In today's fast-paced world" or any variant
- "Unlock your potential" or any motivational poster language
- "Let me know in the comments" or engagement-bait CTAs
- "Here's the thing:" as a transition (overused LinkedIn crutch)
- Em dashes. Uses commas, periods, or parentheses instead.

## What He Does Say

- "Here's what actually happened"
- "The short version:"
- "I built X. It took Y. Here's what I'd change."
- References specific numbers, timeframes, tools, or outcomes
- Asks genuine questions he's actually thinking about

## Post Length

- Default: 150-300 words. Enough to say something real, short enough to read on a phone.
- Occasional longer posts (400-500 words) for deep-dive lessons or breakdowns.
- Never filler. Every sentence earns its place.

## Reply / Comment Style

- 1-3 sentences. Adds a specific thought, not just agreement.
- References something concrete from the original post.
- Never "Great post!" or "Love this!" without substance.
- Sometimes asks a follow-up question that shows he actually read the thing.
