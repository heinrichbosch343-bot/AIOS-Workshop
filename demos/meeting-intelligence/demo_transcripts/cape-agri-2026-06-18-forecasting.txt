Meeting: Cape Agri Cooperative — Demand forecasting scoping
Date: 18 June 2026
Participants: Heinrich (BoschAI), Pieter (Cape Agri Cooperative, Procurement Lead)

Heinrich: Pieter, you mentioned stock planning is where the cooperative bleeds money. Walk me through it.

Pieter: It's guesswork, basically. We order seed, fertiliser, feed — big volumes — based on what we ordered last year plus a gut feel. When we over-order, it sits in the warehouse tying up cash, and some of it expires. When we under-order, members can't get what they need and they buy elsewhere. Both hurt.

Heinrich: Do you have a sense of the number?

Pieter: Conservatively, we're losing a few hundred thousand rand a year between waste and lost sales. Probably more. We've just never measured it properly.

Heinrich: And the data — do you have history of what was sold, when?

Pieter: Years of it. Sales by product, by month, going back well over two years. It's all in the system, we just don't do anything clever with it.

Heinrich: That's actually the good news. With that history, we can forecast demand properly instead of guessing. There's a forecasting model we use that takes your sales history and projects the next few months per product, and it flags when something's trending up or down before it bites you.

Pieter: So it would tell me, going into a season, roughly how much to order?

Heinrich: Yes — and it improves as it sees more. We'd start with a proof of concept on a handful of your highest-value products, show you the forecast against what actually happened, and you judge if it's accurate enough to trust.

Pieter: I'd want to see that before committing. If it's close, this pays for itself in one season.

Heinrich: Agreed. Can you export two years of sales history for your top products? I'll run the forecast and we'll compare it to reality so you can see the accuracy.

Pieter: I'll pull that this week.

Heinrich: Then let's review the results on the 30th.

Pieter: Done. If this works, I want to talk about rolling it across the whole catalogue.
