## Slide 1 — The One-Line Story

You're a young farm doing almost everything right. The one thing missing is a clear view of what to grow and when. That single blind spot is costing you close to **R50,000 every month** — money lost to retail buying plus time burned on manual tracking. Over a year, that's roughly **R600,000**.

This document lays out exactly where that number comes from.

---

## Slide 2 — How the Business Runs Today

A husband-and-wife operation. The split is clean:

- **Husband** runs the growing side — planting, tending, harvesting.
- **Connie** runs everything else — orders, what to buy, deliveries, client management.

The model is a subscription veg box. Clients pay weekly or monthly, you grow the produce, you pack the boxes, you deliver. Right now you're serving **30 to 40 clients a month**.

It works. People pay, boxes go out. But underneath, every week runs on memory and guesswork instead of numbers.

---

## Slide 3 — The Core Problem

You can't see, ahead of time, how much of each vegetable you'll actually need.

So the cycle goes like this. You plant roughly what feels right. Orders come in. Somewhere mid-week you realise you're short on spinach, or tomatoes, or whatever the boxes need. And there's only one fix left at that point: drive to the store and buy retail to fill the gap.

Every one of those store runs costs around **R300**. Worse, you're buying at retail to sell at your own price, so the margin on that produce is gone. You grew it for nothing or you're selling someone else's veg at a loss.

This isn't a farming problem. The husband can grow. It's a **planning problem** — nobody can tell him the right amount to plant because the demand was never calculated.

---

## Slide 4 — Where the Money Leaks

Two leaks, running at the same time.

**Leak 1 — Lost profit.** By your own estimate, the gap between what you make now and what you'd make with the right amounts grown is about **R13,000 a month** in pure profit. Stop the retail buying and that margin comes back. Account for the full swing from where you are now to a clean system and it's closer to **R26,000 a month**.

**Leak 2 — Lost time.** Between working out what's needed, deciding what to buy, sorting deliveries, and putting out fires, Connie spends roughly **20 hours a month** on logistics that a system should handle. At a value of **R1,200 an hour**, that's **R24,000 a month** of her time spent on spreadsheet-and-guesswork work.

## Slide 7 — What We're Building

A dashboard that closes the loop between orders and growing. Four parts, all driven by simple maths:

1. **Orders in.** The system knows your clients, their box type, and what each box contains.
2. **Demand calculated.** It multiplies orders by box contents and tells you exactly how much of each vegetable you need, weeks ahead.
3. **Growing tracked.** Your husband logs what's planted and when it'll be ready.
4. **The gap, in colour.** One screen shows needed vs. available for every vegetable. Green means covered. Red means plant now, or you'll be at the store again.

Plus alerts: *"Plant 8kg of spinach by the 3rd to cover your deliveries on the 28th."*

No more guessing. The plan tells the grower what to plant, and it tells Connie when something's about to fall short — early enough to fix it on the farm instead of at the till.

The build costs between **R20,000 and R30,000**, once off.

Against a problem costing close to **R600,000 a year**, here's what that means:

- It pays for itself in **under three weeks**.

You're not spending money on software. You're buying back R600,000 a year, and getting Connie's time back so she can spend it where it's worth R1,200 an hour.

## Slide 9 — Why Now

The business is small enough that the system is simple to build, and big enough that the leak already hurts. At 30–40 clients the maths is clean. As you grow to 60, 80, 100 clients, the guesswork gets exponentially harder and the lost profit grows with it.

Building it now means you scale on top of a system instead of scaling the chaos.
