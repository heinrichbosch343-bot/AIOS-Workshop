# Re-Engagement Emails — Warm Leads from Sent Mail

These 31 contacts already know you. Most booked a call or replied to you back in
Sept/Oct 2025. That makes this a re-engagement job, not cold outreach. Treat it
that way and it converts far better.

**Send these from your normal Gmail, not Instantly/Apollo.** Cold tools are built
for strangers. These people remember you, so a personal email from your real
inbox lands better and won't trip spam filters. Send in small batches, tweak the
first line per person.

## Template A — Booked a call, then went quiet

Use for everyone whose last subject starts with "Appointment booked". They
scheduled time with you and it slipped. ~18 people.

**Subject:** picking this back up, {{first_name}}?

```
Hi {{first_name}},

Last {{month}} you booked a call with me about automating parts of
{{company}}, and we never closed the loop. That's on me.

I'm writing because the work has moved a long way since then. Back then I
was showing people what AI could do. Now I've built a tool for a client that
cut her document search from two full days down to thirty minutes.

If taking the repetitive load off {{company}} is still worth 15 minutes to
you, I'd love to show you what's actually possible now. If the timing's off,
just say so and I'll leave it.

Heinrich
```

## Template B — Real conversation that fizzled

Use for people you had genuine back-and-forth with (replied, follow-ups, demos
discussed). Reply inside the original Gmail thread so it keeps the history.

**Subject:** Re: [their original subject]

```
Hi {{first_name}},

Circling back on our chat about {{topic}}. I didn't want to let this one
quietly die.

Since we last spoke I've built a tool for a client that cut her document
search from two full days down to thirty minutes. Given where {{company}}
was headed, I reckon it's more useful now than it was then.

Open to 15 minutes to pick it up where we left off?

Heinrich
```

## Template C — The "close your file" follow-up

Send only after A or B gets no reply (wait 4-5 days). This one pulls the most
responses of all three, because it's easy to answer.

**Subject:** should I close your file, {{first_name}}?

```
Hi {{first_name}},

I've reached out a couple of times about automating {{company}} and haven't
heard back. Usually that means the timing's wrong, or it's just not a
priority right now. Both are completely fine.

Reply "later" and I'll check back in a few months. Reply "send it" and I'll
fire over a 2-minute video showing exactly what I'd build for you. Hear
nothing, and I'll leave you in peace.

Heinrich
```
