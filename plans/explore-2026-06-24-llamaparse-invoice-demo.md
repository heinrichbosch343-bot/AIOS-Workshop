# Explore: Paperwork → Data (LlamaParse Invoice Demo + System)

**Created:** 2026-06-24
**Status:** Explored
**Origin:** A quick, visual dashboard + filmable LinkedIn demo showing an invoice turn into a spreadsheet, built like the Drive Intelligence demo, that doubles as a reusable system for our own use and future clients.

## Vision

Drop a stack of messy supplier invoices into a dashboard and watch them become a clean spreadsheet in seconds. It's the second "Voyage move" after the Drive demo: one API (LlamaParse) does the hard reading, we wrap a dead-simple visual story around it ("your paperwork files itself"), and the viewer never hears the word API. The same code we film with becomes a tool we can run for ourselves and sell to clients drowning in paperwork.

## Problem Statement

Every business with suppliers gets a steady stream of invoices as PDFs, scans, and email attachments. Someone retypes them by hand into accounting software: vendor, date, each line item, VAT, total. A small firm doing 1,000 documents a month burns roughly 50-60 hours of admin time on this, plus the cost of every typo (wrong number = payment dispute weeks later). The information already exists on the page. Getting it into a usable form is the job that eats the week.

On camera this is instantly understood. Everyone watching has a drawer or an inbox full of this.

## Proposed Solution

### What It Does

A single-page web dashboard. You drag in one or more invoice files. It reads each one and fills a table on screen with the fields that matter, then lets you download the lot as a spreadsheet (CSV/Excel). Nothing is typed by hand. Nothing is invented; if a field isn't on the page, it's left blank rather than guessed.

### How It Works

1. You open the dashboard (local Streamlit page, same as the Drive demo).
2. You drag 1-5 invoice PDFs onto the upload box.
3. The app sends each file to **LlamaParse Extract** with a fixed schema (the list of fields we want).
4. Extract returns clean, typed data per invoice.
5. The app shows a live table: one row per line item, columns for vendor, invoice number, date, description, quantity, unit price, VAT, total.
6. A "Download spreadsheet" button exports the table as CSV/Excel.

### What It Produces

- An on-screen table that fills in as documents are read (the hero visual for the video).
- A downloadable spreadsheet of every invoice's data.
- A reusable codebase in `outputs/invoice-demo/` that points at fake invoices for the film and real ones for actual use.

## Technical Considerations

### APIs & Keys

| Key | What it's for | Where to get it | Cost |
|-----|---------------|-----------------|------|
| `LLAMA_CLOUD_API_KEY` | Reading invoices, returning structured data | Sign up free at **cloud.llamaindex.ai** (Google/GitHub, no card) → Settings → API Keys → Create Key | Free tier: **10,000 credits/month**, resets monthly. Extract is ~6 credits/page on the cost-effective setting, so 1,000+ pages/month free. A demo uses a handful. |

No other new keys. We do not need Anthropic, Voyage, or Supabase for the MVP.

### Libraries

- `llama-cloud` (current SDK; the older `llama-cloud-services` is being retired)
- `streamlit` (already in `.venv`)
- `pandas` + `openpyxl` (table handling + Excel export)

Install into the existing `.venv` the same way we fixed the Drive demo.

### How the Extract call works (plain English)

We hand LlamaParse two things: the invoice file, and a short description of the fields we want back (a "schema"). It returns those fields filled in, typed correctly (dates as dates, amounts as numbers). Our schema for invoices:

- vendor name
- invoice number
- invoice date
- line items (description, quantity, unit price, line total)
- subtotal, VAT, total

### Cost reality

The demo costs effectively nothing (well inside the free 10k credits). Even running it as a real tool for ourselves stays free until we're processing well over a thousand pages a month. If a client needs more, the paid tier is cheap relative to the admin hours it replaces (the selling point).

### Risks / honest flags

- **Parse takes a few seconds per file.** Not instant. We design the demo beat around a short narrated wait, same as the Drive demo's "reading your documents" moment.
- **Streamlit upload** is reliable but we test the drag-drop on the actual machine before filming so there's no surprise on camera.
- **Sample realism** matters. Fake invoices need real-looking layouts (logo block, line-item table, totals) or the demo looks staged. We generate proper PDFs, not plain text.

## The Film Plan (LinkedIn post)

A 60-90 second clip. Same arc that worked for the Drive demo: problem → watch it happen → the trust beat → possibilities.

1. **Hook (5s):** "Someone in your business is retyping this by hand right now." Hold up / show a messy invoice. "Watch me skip that entirely."
2. **The pain (10s):** the admin-hours story. "A thousand invoices a month is fifty hours of typing. The numbers are already on the page. Typing them is the job that eats the week."
3. **The demo (20s):** drag 3 invoices onto the dashboard. Narrate the short read. The table fills in. "Three suppliers, every line item, every total. I typed nothing."
4. **The trust beat (10s):** point at a blank field. "See this? It left it blank instead of guessing. If it's not on the invoice, it won't invent it. That's why you can trust it with money."
5. **Download moment (5s):** click "Download spreadsheet." "Done. That's in my accounting system in one click."
6. **Possibilities (15s):** "This is invoices. Point it at receipts, delivery notes, contracts. Any paperwork, any business."
7. **CTA (10s):** "If your team retypes documents, I build this in a day. Comment PAPER or message me and I'll show you on your files."

Filming notes: use the generated fake invoices (camera-safe), test upload before rolling, fill the read-wait with narration, pause a full beat on the blank-field trust moment.
